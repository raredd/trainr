All analyses were performed using R version 3.2.0 (2015-04-16) and packages survival v2.38.1 for time-to-event analyses; and knitr v1.10.5 for reproducible research.

Summary

The primary outcome was - overall response rate (ORR)

The secondary outcomes were: - rate of PR, CR or better - progression-free survival - overall survival

Some patients responsed and there were some toxicities. This summay should be written better.

Statistical considerations

Responses to study treatment were reported as proportions with 95% exact binomial confidence intervals. Time-to-event endpoints were summarized using the Kaplan-Meier method with variance estimated using the log-log transformation.

Administrative Information

From April 2012 to May 2013, 35 patients were enrolled on study at MGH (n = 14, 40%), BWH (n = 11, 31%), and DFCI (n = 10, 29%). As per inclusion criteria, all patients were newly diagnosed diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL). By study design patients were stratified by subtype, and all results were performed as such.

Thirty-five (100% of) patients received at least one dose of study treatment and were included in toxicity and response analyses.

Patient Characteristics

Patient characteristics are presented in Table 1. Seventeen (49% of) patients enrolled were male. The median age at diagnosis was 67 (range: 38 - 89). Twenty-eight (80% of) patients had International Prognostic Index (IPI) of two or more.

Response to treatment

The primary outcome was overall response rate (MR, PR, CR) at end of treatment. All patients received at least one dose of study treatment and were included in response assessment, and all patients were evaluated for EOT response.

The overall response rate of MR or better was 66% (95% CI: 48 - 81%). Sixteen (46% of) patients experienced a PR or better at EOT (95% CI: 29 - 63%). Four (11% of) patients experienced a CR or better (95% CI: 3 - 27%).
